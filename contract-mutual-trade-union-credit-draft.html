1. CREDIT FACILITY

Party A hereby grants Party B a revolving credit facility in the amount of 6,000 USD (United States Dollars) exclusively for the shipment in accordance with confirmed orders. This credit facility is subject to review, adjustment, and conditions as specified herein. Each invoice issued by Party A constitutes a discrete credit event under this Agreement. This credit facility is subject to the conditions as specified herein.

2. CREDIT LIMIT REVIEW

The credit limit shall be subject to a mandatory review every sixty (60) calendar days from the Effective Date of this Agreement. Party A reserves the sole and absolute right to increase or decrease the credit limit at its discretion based on Party B's payment history, order volume, and overall performance. Party A shall provide Party B with written notice of any adjustment to the credit limit within five (5) Business Days prior to the adjustment taking effect.

3. PAYMENT SCHEDULE

Party B agrees to settle all outstanding invoices issued by Party A in accordance with the payment schedule selected below. Payments shall be made in full, in USD, to Party A's designated bank account. The applicable payment schedule for this Agreement is indicated below:

  1. Party B purchases all products by themselves and agrees to pay Party A the fee (shipping fee and service fee) once a week on Thursday following the invoice order list provided by Party A.
  2. For storage fees: the first month is free. If Party B goods shipped less than 50% of inventory after each one month, corresponding warehouse fees will apply.

All payments shall reference the invoice number issued by Party A. Failure to make payment on the agreed schedule shall constitute a payment default as described in Section 5.

4. INVENTORY OBLIGATIONS & PRODUCT DISCONTINUATION

All product inventory is purchased directly by Party B at its own cost and discretion; Party A bears no responsibility for Party B's inventory holdings or any unsold stock. In the event that Party B decides to discontinue the sale of any product(s), Party B shall provide Party A with a minimum of fourteen (14) calendar days' prior written notice of such discontinuation. Any outstanding amounts owed to Party A for previously delivered inventory shall remain due and be settled in accordance with the payment terms set out in Section 5 of this Agreement, regardless of whether such inventory has been sold by Party B.

5. LATE PAYMENT & PENALTIES

5.1 Interest on Overdue Amounts:

Any outstanding amount not received by Party A on the agreed payment date shall accrue interest at a rate of 1.5% per month (or part thereof) calculated on the total overdue balance, commencing from the first day following the due date until full payment is received.

5.2 Credit Suspension:

In the event that any payment remains outstanding for more than seven (7) calendar days beyond the agreed payment due date, Party A reserves the right to immediately suspend Party B's credit facility without further notice. Suspension shall remain in effect until all overdue amounts, including accrued interest and penalties, are paid in full to Party A's designated bank account.

6. CONTRACT DURATION & AUTOMATIC RENEWAL

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and renew automatically on an annual basis unless terminated under Section 8. Party A shall give Party B at least sixty (60) days' written notice before each Chinese New Year (Lunar New Year) specifying the payment due date and amount outstanding. Party B shall settle all outstanding balances no later than five (5) Business Days before such date; failure to do so shall constitute a payment default.

7. CONFIDENTIALITY

Both parties acknowledge and agree that all information exchanged under this Agreement, including but not limited to pricing, credit terms, product details, business strategies, supplier information, and financial data, is strictly confidential. Neither party shall disclose, publish, or share such information with any third party without the prior written consent of the other party. This confidentiality obligation shall survive the termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of three (3) years.

8. TERMINATION

8.1 Termination by Notice:

Either party may terminate this Agreement by providing the other party with a minimum of thirty (30) calendar days' prior written notice. Upon termination, all outstanding balances owed to Party A shall become immediately due and payable.

8.2 Immediate Termination by Party A:

Party A reserves the right to terminate this Agreement immediately and without prior notice in the event that Party B: (i) fails to make payment on the agreed schedule; (ii) becomes insolvent or enters into bankruptcy proceedings; (iii) breaches any material term of this Agreement and fails to remedy such breach within seven (7) Business Days of written notice from Party A. Upon immediate termination, all outstanding amounts shall become due and payable within three (3) Business Days.

9. DISPUTE RESOLUTION

9.1 Amicable Negotiation:

Any dispute, controversy, or claim arising out of or in connection with this Agreement shall first be referred to good-faith negotiation between the authorized representatives of both parties. The parties shall endeavor to resolve the dispute within thirty (30) calendar days from the date of written notification of the dispute.

9.2 Arbitration:

If the dispute is not resolved through negotiation within the prescribed period, or in the case of non-payment where Party A may elect to bypass negotiation and proceed directly to legal action, the dispute shall be submitted to arbitration administered by the Singapore International Arbitration Centre (SIAC) in accordance with its then-current Arbitration Rules. The arbitration shall be seated in Singapore and conducted in English by (3) arbitrators. The arbitral award shall be final and binding on both parties.

10. GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the People's Republic of China, without regard to its conflict of law principles.

11. KYC & REQUIRED DOCUMENTATION

As a condition precedent to the activation of the credit facility, Party B shall provide Party A with the following mandatory documents prior to or upon signing of this Agreement. Party A reserves the right to withhold or suspend the credit facility until all required documents are received and verified.

11.1 Passport Copy:

A clear and valid copy of the passport of the authorized representative of Party B signing this Agreement. The passport must be valid for a minimum of six (6) months from the date of signing and must clearly show the photo page and all personal details.

11.2 Company Registration Certificate:

A valid official copy of Party B's Company Registration Certificate issued by the relevant government authority in Party B's country of registration. The document must clearly state the company name, registration number, registered address, and date of incorporation.

11.3 Document Acknowledgment:

By signing this Agreement, Party B confirms all submitted documents are authentic, accurate, and up to date. All documents shall be treated as strictly confidential per Section 7. Party B must notify Party A immediately of any changes.

12. GENERAL PROVISIONS

12.1 Entire Agreement: This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the parties and supersedes all prior negotiations, representations, or agreements.

12.2 Amendments: Any amendment or modification to this Agreement must be made in writing and signed by authorized representatives of both parties.

12.3 Severability: If any provision of this Agreement is found to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

12.4 Notices: All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and delivered via email or registered mail to the addresses specified by each party.

12.5 Force Majeure: Neither party shall be liable for any failure or delay in performance due to causes beyond its reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, pandemics, war, or government actions. Force majeure shall not excuse or delay any payment obligations under this Agreement.

13. DISPATCH TIMELINE

Party A shall process and dispatch all outbound shipments within three (3) Business Days of receiving a written dispatch instruction from Party B. Upon dispatch, Party A shall provide Party B with the tracking number, carrier name, and estimated transit time within one (1) Business Day. In the event of a dispatch delay beyond the agreed timeline caused by Party A, Party B shall be entitled to a credit note equal to 1% of the affected shipment value per Business Day of delay, up to a maximum of 10% of that shipment value.

14. ORDER ACCURACY

Party A shall be responsible for accurate picking, packing, and dispatch of all outbound orders. Each shipment shall match the SKUs and quantities specified in the written dispatch instruction from Party B. Party A shall maintain accurate inventory records and provide a current stock report to Party B within two (2) Business Days upon written request. In the event of a fulfillment error attributable to Party A, Party A shall bear all costs of correction — including reshipping — or shall issue a credit note at the original invoice value within five (5) Business Days of a written claim from Party B.

15. SHIPPING DOCUMENTATION

Party A shall be responsible for the accuracy and completeness of all outbound shipping documentation prepared by Party A, including commercial invoices, packing lists, and export declarations. If errors in documentation prepared by Party A result in customs delays, fines, or directly attributable additional costs incurred by Party B, Party A shall reimburse all documented direct costs within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of a written claim supported by relevant documentation.
