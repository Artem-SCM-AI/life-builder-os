We thank you for the updated Agreement and confirm our readiness to proceed. Before execution, we wish to propose three additional clauses covering operational service standards. These are not expressions of distrust — they formalize the mutual expectations that any professional fulfillment partnership operates on, and protect both parties equally.

We respectfully request your consideration of the following additions.

---

**Amendment A — New Section 13: Dispatch Timeline**

*Proposed:*

> Party A shall process and dispatch all outbound shipments within three (3) Business Days of receiving a written dispatch instruction from Party B. Upon dispatch, Party A shall provide Party B with the tracking number, carrier name, and estimated transit time within one (1) Business Day. In the event of a dispatch delay beyond the agreed timeline caused by Party A, Party B shall be entitled to a credit note equal to 1% of the affected shipment value per Business Day of delay, up to a maximum of 10% of that shipment value.

**Rationale:** Dispatch speed is the core deliverable of a fulfillment partner. A clear timeline with a proportionate remedy ensures operational predictability for Party B's downstream supply chain.

---

**Amendment B — New Section 14: Order Accuracy**

*Proposed:*

> Party A shall be responsible for accurate picking, packing, and dispatch of all outbound orders. Each shipment shall match the SKUs and quantities specified in the written dispatch instruction from Party B. Party A shall maintain accurate inventory records and provide a current stock report to Party B within two (2) Business Days upon written request. In the event of a fulfillment error attributable to Party A, Party A shall bear all costs of correction — including reshipping — or shall issue a credit note at the original invoice value within five (5) Business Days of a written claim from Party B.

**Rationale:** Incorrect shipments damage Party B's end-customer relationships and generate return costs. This clause sets a clear, fair standard and provides a resolution path when errors occur.

---

**Amendment C — New Section 15: Shipping Documentation**

*Proposed:*

> Party A shall be responsible for the accuracy and completeness of all outbound shipping documentation prepared by Party A, including commercial invoices, packing lists, and export declarations. If errors in documentation prepared by Party A result in customs delays, fines, or directly attributable additional costs incurred by Party B, Party A shall reimburse all documented direct costs within ten (10) Business Days of receipt of a written claim supported by relevant documentation.

**Rationale:** Shipments from China to European destinations are subject to customs clearance. Documentation errors cause costly delays and regulatory exposure. As the party preparing these documents, Party A must be accountable for their accuracy.

---

We are confident these three additions reflect standard practice for any professional fulfillment operation and will serve as a clear, fair framework for both parties. We remain fully available to discuss the wording at your convenience.
