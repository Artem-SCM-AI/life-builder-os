**LEAD-GEN-SYSTEM.md** — Daily lead generation playbook:

- Section 1: ICP definition (Ideal Customer Profile)
  - Amazon brand owner, 5–50 SKUs, China-sourced, using FBA + 3PL
  - Revenue range: $500K–$10M/yr
  - Signals: active on LinkedIn, posts about supply chain/Amazon operations, uses phrases like "stockout", "3PL headaches", "supplier delays"

- Section 2: LinkedIn Search Filters (exact filter strings to use in Sales Navigator or regular LinkedIn)
  - Title filters: "Head of Supply Chain", "Supply Chain Manager", "Operations Manager", "Amazon Brand Owner", "E-commerce Founder", "FBA Seller"
  - Industry: Retail, Consumer Goods, Wholesale
  - Company size: 1–50 employees
  - Geography: US, UK, Canada, Australia (English-speaking Amazon markets)
  - Keyword filter: "FBA" OR "Amazon seller" OR "China supplier" OR "3PL"

- Section 3: Daily workflow (15–20 min/day execution)
  1. Open LinkedIn, apply saved search filters
  2. Review 30–40 profiles, qualify against ICP (2 must-haves: sells on Amazon, has supply chain operations)
  3. Add 20–30 qualified leads to the LEAD-TRACKER Google Sheet (Name, Profile URL, Company, Title, ICP signal noted)
  4. Send 10–15 warm or cold DMs using scripts from WARM-OUTREACH-SCRIPTS.md or COLD-OUTREACH-SCRIPTS.md
  5. Log all sent messages in tracker (Status: "Contacted - Cold" or "Contacted - Warm")
  6. Review replies, respond same day, move stage to "Replied" or "Call Booked"

- Section 4: Lead volume math
  - 20–30 new leads/day × 5 days/week = 100–150 leads/week
  - Expected reply rate: 5–10% cold, 30–50% warm
  - Expected call booking rate from replies: 40–60%
  - Target: 2–3 calls booked per week minimum

- Section 5: Warm contact priority list (fill in manually before starting)
  - Tier 1: Ex-Jumpzylla colleagues and contacts (highest trust)
  - Tier 2: LinkedIn connections with SC/Amazon background already connected
  - Tier 3: SCAI customers from Phase 1 (warmest upsell leads for Products 2–5)
  - Note: Approach warm contacts first — aim for 2–3 calls from warm before scaling cold outreach

**LEAD-TRACKER-SPEC.md** — Google Sheets structure:

Tab 1: "Lead Pipeline"
Columns (A through L):
- A: Lead ID (auto: LEAD-001, LEAD-002...)
- B: Full Name
- C: LinkedIn URL
- D: Company
- E: Title
- F: ICP Signal (brief note: e.g., "posts about 3PL issues", "FBA brand, 12 SKUs")
- G: Lead Source (Warm-Colleague / Warm-Connection / Warm-SCAI / Cold-LinkedIn / Cold-Email)
- H: Product Interest (DS / EMS / DEVMON / SINV / 3PLV — which product to pitch first)
- I: Status (dropdown: New → Contacted → Replied → Call Booked → Demo Done → Offer Made → Closed Won → Closed Lost → Follow Up Later)
- J: Date Contacted
- K: Date of Last Activity
- L: Notes (free text)

Tab 2: "Interview Log" (linked to 02-03 pipeline — one row per completed call)
Columns:
- A: Lead ID (links to Tab 1)
- B: Call Date
- C: Product Demoed
- D: Key Pain Discovered
- E: Offer Made (Y/N)
- F: Outcome (Paid / LOI / Not Now / No Fit)
- G: Follow-up Action

Tab 3: "MRR Dashboard" (summary)
- Total leads in pipeline
- Calls booked this week
- Calls completed this month
- Total paying customers
- MRR formula: =COUNTIF(Tab1!I:I,"Closed Won") * 49 (assumes $49/mo per customer)
- Formula for setup revenue: =COUNTIF(Tab1!I:I,"Closed Won") * 300

Note at bottom of LEAD-TRACKER-SPEC.md: "Build this in Google Sheets. Share link with eco.stepanenko@gmail.com. Bookmark the sheet."
