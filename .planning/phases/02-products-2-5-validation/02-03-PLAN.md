**INTERVIEW-MASTER-SCRIPT.md** — Universal call structure for all 40 interviews:

- Section 1: Timing table

| Block | Time | What happens |
|-------|------|--------------|
| Opening | 1 min | Thank them, state agenda: "I want to understand your current process, then show you something specific, then if it makes sense we can talk next steps" |
| Discovery | 5–7 min | Ask 3–4 product-specific questions (see each product script); listen, don't pitch |
| Demo | 4–5 min | Open Claude, paste demo prompt + sample data, show output live; 1–2 key observations |
| Offer | 2–3 min | State price, state what setup includes, ask close question |
| Close/Next | 1 min | Either get "yes" or book follow-up |
| **Total** | **~15 min** | |

- Section 2: Universal opening line (exact words)
"Thanks for making time. I'll be direct — I want to spend 5 minutes understanding how you currently handle [product topic], then show you a specific tool I've built, and you tell me if it's relevant. Sound good?"

- Section 3: Universal offer close (exact words, used after demo)
"Here's how it works: $300 setup — that's where I map your data sources and configure everything for your specific SKUs, suppliers, and 3PLs. Then $49/month for weekly delivery. If I save you 2 hours per week, you've paid for the month in day one. Want to move forward?"

- Section 4: Objection responses
  - "I need to think about it": "Totally fair. What's the main thing you'd need to be confident about?" (then address it directly)
  - "It's too expensive": "What are you spending now on fixing data errors or chasing supplier info? This is probably less than one hour of your time per month."
  - "I have a system already": "What does it do when the data is wrong or missing? That's the gap this fills."
  - "Not right now": "When would be right? I'll follow up then. Can I send you a 90-second demo video in the meantime?"

- Section 5: Call log template (copy for each completed call)
```
Call Log — [Date] | [Prospect Name] | [Product Pitched]
Pain confirmed: [yes/no] | Exact pain stated: [quote]
Demo reaction: [positive/neutral/negative] | Specific comment: [quote]
Offer made: [yes/no] | Response: [accepted/declined/follow-up date]
Outcome: [Closed Won / LOI / Not Now / No Fit]
Follow-up action: [what, by when]
Interview number: [X of 10 for this product]
```

**DEMO-PREP-GUIDE.md** — Pre-call checklist and in-call demo steps for all 4 products:

Structure: One section per product (EMS, DEVMON, SINV, 3PLV).

For each product:

**EMS Section:**
- Pre-call prep (2 min before call): Open a text file with EMS demo sample data ready to paste
- Sample data to prepare: A table with 5 SKUs, showing: SKU name, current inventory (units), last 7-day sales velocity, days of stock remaining, reorder point. Include 2 SKUs where days_of_stock < reorder_point (stockout risk) and 1 SKU where inventory > 3x reorder point (overstock).
- Demo prompt summary: "Paste this table + the EMS analysis prompt into Claude → it will output a ranked issue list with severity scores (Critical / Warning / OK)"
- What to say during demo: "This took 45 seconds. Normally you'd need to scan this manually or build pivot tables. The AI ranks by severity so you know exactly what to act on first."

**DEVMON Section:**
- Sample data: A table with 4 SKUs showing: SKU, week, forecasted units, actual units sold. Include 2 SKUs with >15% deviation (one under, one over).
- Demo prompt summary: "Paste forecast vs actual table + DEVMON prompt → Claude outputs deviation report with root-cause signals"
- What to say: "It doesn't just show the gap — it surfaces the likely reason: was it a promo, a stockout, a seasonality spike? That's the part that takes you hours to diagnose manually."

**SINV Section:**
- Sample data: A 3-row comparison: PO (qty 500 units @ $12.00/unit), Supplier Invoice (qty 500 units @ $12.40/unit + $150 "handling fee"), Receiving Record (qty 488 units received).
- Demo prompt summary: "Paste PO + invoice + receiving record + SINV prompt → Claude outputs 3-way match discrepancy report"
- What to say: "Found two issues in 20 seconds: $0.40/unit price variance ($200 total) and 12 units short on receipt ($144 unbilled). Most brands don't catch these until month-end, if at all."

**3PLV Section:**
- Sample data: A 3PL invoice with 4 line items (pallet storage, inbound, outbound, labeling). One line has a charge 20% above the rate card, one line charges for 50 pallets when receipt shows 42.
- Demo prompt summary: "Paste 3PL invoice + contract rate card + 3PLV prompt → Claude outputs variance report with dollar impact"
- What to say: "This client had a $340 overcharge on one invoice. Across a year that's thousands of dollars. Claude catches it in 60 seconds."

**EMS-SALES-SCRIPT.md** specifics:
- Workflow summary: "Client provides inventory + sales data → Claude analyzes → ranked issue list with stockout/overstock/delay severity scores → delivered as Google Sheet or doc"
- Discovery questions:
  1. "How do you currently know when a SKU is heading toward a stockout? Walk me through what you check."
  2. "How many times last year did you actually run out of stock on a fast-moving SKU? What did it cost you?"
  3. "Do you have a threshold system right now — like, do you get alerts when inventory drops below X days?"
  4. "If you had a ranked list every Monday morning showing your 3 highest-risk SKUs, what would you do with it?"
- Demo anchor line: "This ranked list — Critical / Warning / OK — took 45 seconds. You were doing this manually in pivot tables, or not doing it at all."
- ROI anchor: "One stockout on a $30 product selling 50/day is $1,500 in lost revenue per day. Catching it 5 days earlier pays for this service for 2+ years."

**DEVMON-SALES-SCRIPT.md** specifics:
- Workflow summary: "Client provides forecast vs actual sales data by SKU and period → Claude compares and surfaces deviations → report shows variance %, direction (over/under), and root-cause signals"
- Discovery questions:
  1. "Do you track demand forecasts for your SKUs? How are they set — gut feel, historical data, or something else?"
  2. "When actual sales are way off forecast, how do you find out — and how long does it take?"
  3. "What decisions go wrong when your forecast is off? Think about inventory orders, promotions, production runs."
  4. "If you got a weekly report showing which SKUs deviated >10% from forecast and why, what would change?"
- Demo anchor line: "Two SKUs flagged: one sold 40% below forecast — that's overstock building. One sold 30% above — you're heading into a stockout. Both spotted in 30 seconds."
- ROI anchor: "Most brands catch demand deviations at month-end, after the inventory decision is already wrong. This gives you a 2–3 week head start to adjust."

**SINV-SALES-SCRIPT.md** specifics:
- Workflow summary: "Client provides PO + supplier invoice + receiving record → Claude runs 3-way match → discrepancy report shows price variances, quantity mismatches, and unauthorized charges"
- Discovery questions:
  1. "How do you verify that what your supplier charged you matches what you ordered and received?"
  2. "Have you ever discovered a supplier invoice error after already paying? How did you handle it?"
  3. "How much time does your team spend on AP reconciliation each month?"
  4. "If you could automatically flag any invoice that doesn't match the PO before payment, how much would that save you?"
- Demo anchor line: "Found a $200 price variance and 12 units short on receipt — in 20 seconds. If you pay this invoice as-is, you're out $344 you shouldn't be."
- ROI anchor: "Suppliers overbill by 2–5% on average when there's no systematic check. On $500K in annual supplier spend, that's $10,000–$25,000 in recoverable overcharges."

**3PLV-SALES-SCRIPT.md** specifics:
- Workflow summary: "Client provides 3PL invoice + signed contract rate card → Claude compares line by line → variance report shows overcharges, unauthorized fees, and dollar impact per line"
- Discovery questions:
  1. "Do you manually check your 3PL invoices against your contract rates? How long does that take?"
  2. "Has your 3PL ever charged you for something that wasn't in the contract — extra handling, rounding up pallets, mystery fees?"
  3. "How many 3PL invoices do you process per month, and what's the average dollar value?"
  4. "If you knew exactly which line items were overbilled before you paid, what's the first thing you'd do?"
- Demo anchor line: "Two discrepancies: $0.50/pallet overcharge on 50 pallets ($25), and 8 extra pallets billed that don't appear on your receiving record ($88). Total: $113 on one invoice."
- ROI anchor: "I've seen 3PL overbilling rates of 3–8% on mid-sized brands. On a $5,000/month 3PL bill, that's $150–$400 per month in recoverable charges. This service pays for itself in the first invoice."
