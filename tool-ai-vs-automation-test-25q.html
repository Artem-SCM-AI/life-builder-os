AI or Automation?

25-question decision test · ~8 minutes

When people ask "AI or automation?" they usually mean "which is better?" That's the wrong question. One costs 3–5× more per operation, handles uncertainty, and makes probabilistic decisions. The other is faster, cheaper, and 100% predictable — as long as the input is exactly what it expects. Neither is better. They solve different problems.

The decision depends entirely on the specific task you're evaluating — not your company size, not your industry, not your general attitude toward technology. Pick one task. Think about how it actually works right now. Answer 25 questions about that task.

A higher score means the task leans toward classic automation. A lower score means it requires AI. The breakdown by block will show you exactly which dimensions are driving that result.

Be precise. Tasks that seem "AI-ready" often turn out to be automatable with basic tooling. Tasks that seem simple often have edge cases that break pure automation in production. Both mistakes are expensive.

Why the decision matters

Using AI where automation would work adds 3–5× cost per transaction with no meaningful benefit — and introduces probabilistic errors into a task that has deterministic answers (Appian, 2024)
Most RPA and automation projects that fail do so because they were applied to the wrong task type — judgment-heavy work that rule-based tools can't handle reliably (UiPath, 2024)
Intelligent automation — combining AI and RPA in the right architecture — consistently delivers higher ROI than either tool alone, but only when the split between them is designed deliberately (UiPath, 2024)
The majority of business processes contain both automatable and AI-dependent components — the question is never "AI vs automation" globally, always for the specific task at hand (Latenode, 2025)
AI agents can now handle multi-step reasoning and adaptive decision-making that was previously impossible to automate — but they require different architecture, governance, and error-handling than classic RPA (TechTarget, 2025)
