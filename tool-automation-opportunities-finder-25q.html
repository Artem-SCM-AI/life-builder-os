What Can I Automate in My Work?

25-question personal audit · ~8 minutes · works for any profession

Most people know they're doing too much manually. What they don't know is where exactly their time is going — and which of those tasks are actually automatable right now, without technical skills or big budgets.

This is a personal audit, not a company assessment. Think about your typical workweek — everything you do regularly. Rate each question honestly: how often does this happen in your work?

The result shows you which category of manual work is costing you the most time. That category is your starting point. You'll also see how each area scores — so you can prioritize what to tackle in what order.

Works for any profession: business owner, manager, freelancer, specialist, or anyone with a repeating workload. No technical background required to use the results.

Why it matters

Knowledge workers spend 28% of their workweek managing email alone — the majority of which is routine responses and coordination that can be templated or automated (McKinsey Global Institute)
58% of workers' time is spent on "work about work" — status updates, follow-ups, data entry between tools — not the actual skilled work they were hired for (Asana Anatomy of Work Index)
The most common reason companies don't automate is not budget or skill — it's that they don't know where to start. A clear map of which tasks qualify changes this immediately (Workato State of Business Automation)
Small business owners spend an average of 10–20 hours per week on administrative tasks that have direct automation equivalents available with existing tools (OnDeck Business Report)
"If I do it myself it's faster" is the most common rationalization for keeping manual work. At weekly repetition, it costs 10× more than setting up the automation once
