# Knowledge Base 3 — Content Formats & Post Structures
*Distilled from 40 research sources. For Claude Projects — Threads Content Machine.*

---

## Threads Post Fundamentals

**Length:** 100–300 words. Sweet spot: 150–200.
**Paragraphs:** 1–2 sentences max per paragraph. White space is content.
**One idea per post.** Never cram two messages into one post.
**End with direction:** a question, a CTA, or a landing statement. Never a summary.

---

## The 10-Post Weekly Mix (for Artem's funnel)

Out of every 10 posts:
- **6 Pain/Awareness posts** — no direct sell. Build recognition of the problem.
- **3 Solution/Value posts** — show the mechanism. Soft CTA to $9 product.
- **1 Direct offer post** — explicit product promotion.

This ratio keeps the feed valuable, not spammy. Breaking it (too many CTAs) kills reach.

## 7 Post Formats (With Templates)

---

### Format 1 — Pain + Number + Direction
**When to use:** Cold audience. Free Test CTA.
**Structure:**
- Line 1: State the pain as a number
- Lines 2–4: Explain why it's a systems problem, not a you problem
- Lines 5–7: Describe what the automated version looks like
- Closing: Soft CTA or question

**Template:**
```
[Number] hours/week. That's what [role] typically spend on [painful task].

Not because they're bad at it.
Because [root cause explanation].

[Specific consequence of not fixing it.]

→ [What changes when you automate]
→ [Second thing that changes]
→ [Third thing]

[Soft CTA or question]
```

**Example:**
```
10 hours/week. That's what the average job seeker spends searching manually.

Not because they're bad at it.
Because job boards weren't built for people who already have jobs.

The first 70% of applications arrive within 24 hours of posting.
Manual checking once a day means you're always late.

→ An automated agent checks every morning
→ Scores each match against your role, salary, location
→ You wake up to a ranked list

How many hours is your job search costing you this week?
Free calculator — link in bio.
```

### Format 2 — Before → After → Tool
**When to use:** Warm audience. $9 product CTA.
**Structure:**
- BEFORE block (3–4 bullets of the pain state)
- AFTER block (3–4 bullets of the fixed state)
- One sentence naming the tool
- CTA

**Template:**
```
BEFORE:
→ [Pain 1]
→ [Pain 2]
→ [Pain 3]

AFTER:
→ [Result 1]
→ [Result 2]
→ [Result 3]

[One sentence on what made the difference.]

[CTA]
```

### Format 3 — List of 3 ("Which one is you?")
**When to use:** Engagement-first posts. Algorithm feed. No hard CTA needed.
**Structure:**
- Opening: "3 [types/reasons/mistakes]..."
- Three specific, recognizable items
- Closing: "Which one are you?" or "Drop your number below."

**Why it works:** Specific enough to be recognizable. Easy to reply with "1" or "number 2." Replies are the strongest algorithm signal.

**Example:**
```
3 reasons job searches take longer than they should:

1. You check job boards manually — which means you see yesterday's listings today.
2. You apply in batches, not daily — which means you're always late to the best roles.
3. You're tracking 20 applications in your head — which means you forget to follow up.

Which one is costing you the most?
```

### Format 4 — The "I changed my mind" post
**When to use:** Authority building. Engagement. No hard CTA.
**Structure:**
- "I used to think [old belief]"
- "Then [specific event or insight that changed it]"
- "Now I know [new belief]"
- Implication for the reader

**Example:**
```
I used to think job searching was about having the best resume.

Then I tracked my applications for a month.

Roles with the fastest responses had one thing in common: I applied within hours of posting.

Not days. Hours.

The resume matters. But speed matters more.

The best resume sent on day 3 loses to a good resume sent on day 1.
```

### Format 5 — The Case / Scenario (anonymous)
**When to use:** Empathy-building. Warm audience. $9 or $47 CTA.
**Structure:**

- Describe the problem behavior
- Show what changed (the automated version)
- CTA

**Example:**
```
IT lead at a mid-size company.

Wants to leave. Has wanted to leave for 8 months.

Checks LinkedIn twice a week. Usually during lunch or right before bed when the anxiety kicks in.

Applies to 2–3 roles when motivation is high. Forgets to follow up on most.

Here's what the automated version of this week looks like:

Monday morning: 11 new jobs in Notion. Scored. Ranked. Agent ran at 7am.
Monday lunchtime: Reviews the top 3. Applies to 2.
Tuesday: Agent runs again. 4 new roles.

Same person. Different system.

30 min of setup. Then it runs itself. $9 — link in bio.
```

### Format 6 — Objection Killer
**When to use:** Warm/hot audience. Pre-close. Addresses the hesitation that's blocking purchase.
**Structure:**
- State the objection directly ("You might be thinking...")
- Respond specifically and honestly (not defensively)
- Add one proof point or clarification
- Soft CTA

**Common objections for Artem's product:**
- "What if it applies to jobs I don't want?" → It finds, not applies. You decide.
- "Won't my employer know I'm looking?" → Runs on your Mac. No connection to your work accounts.
- "I don't know how to set up tech tools" → 30 minutes, setup guide included, no coding.
- "Is it legal?" → Mimics human browsing. Fully compliant.
- "What if I miss good jobs it didn't find?" → Daily digest. You review before any decision.

### Format 7 — Direct Offer Post (1x per week max)
**When to use:** Hot audience. Explicit product promotion.
**Structure:**
- Who it's NOT for (screens out wrong people — builds trust)
- Who it IS for (self-qualification)
- What they get (specific, not vague)
- Price + one CTA

**Example:**

## Engagement Tactics (In-Post)

**To generate replies:**
- End with "which one is yours?" / "which one costs you the most?" / "what's your number?"
- Give 2–3 specific options to choose from (easier to reply to than open questions)
- Ask about their specific situation, not a generic topic

**To generate reposts:**
- Posts that name an uncomfortable truth people recognize
- Lists of 3 that are "so true it hurts"
- Contrarian takes with clear logic

**To generate profile taps / follows:**
- Every post should implicitly answer: "why would I follow this person?"
- The answer should be: "because they understand exactly what I'm going through and have useful things to say about it."
