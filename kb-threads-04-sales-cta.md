# Knowledge Base 4 — Sales Strategy & CTAs on Threads
*Distilled from 40 research sources. For Claude Projects — Threads Content Machine.*

---

## The Core Insight: Threads Sells Through Conversation

Threads is not a broadcast channel. Hard daily pitching kills reach and trust.

What works: **contextual selling** — offers woven into value posts, where the CTA feels like a natural next step, not an interruption.

**The ratio that works:**
- 60% of posts: pure value/pain (no CTA or only a very soft link-in-bio mention)
- 30% of posts: solution-oriented (soft $9 product CTA)
- 10% of posts: direct offer (explicit promotion)

## The Product Ladder on Threads

**Step 1 — Free Test (cold audience, zero friction)**
Goal: get them to feel their pain as a number.
CTA: "Find out how many hours your job search is stealing from you. Free calculator — link in bio."
When to use: first 2 weeks. Every awareness post. Cold audience only.

**Step 2 — $9 Job Search Agent (warm audience)**
Goal: convert people who've acknowledged the problem.
CTA options:
- "30 minutes of setup. Runs every morning. $9 — link in bio."
- "Stop searching manually. The agent does it for you. $9 one-time — link in bio."
- "If this sounds like your week — the $9 agent fixes it. Link in bio."
When to use: weeks 3–4. Solution posts. People who've engaged before.

**Step 3 — $47 Job Search Orchestrator (hot audience)**
Goal: convert people who want the full system.
CTA options:
- "Full system. Runs while you sleep. $47 one-time — link in bio."
- "If $9 was a taste — the full orchestrator is $47. Link in bio."
When to use: week 5+. Direct offer posts. After establishing credibility.

## CTA Rules

**1. One CTA per post.** Never give two options. The reader who has to choose does nothing.

**2. Always put the link in bio, not the post.** Links in posts reduce organic reach. "Link in bio" is the standard phrase.

**3. Name the action, not the product.** 
Bad: "Check out my Job Search Agent"
Good: "Find out your number — link in bio"
Good: "30 minutes of setup. Runs every morning. Link in bio."

**4. Match CTA to audience temperature.**
Cold → free test CTA (no money ask)
Warm → $9 product CTA (small commitment)
Hot → $47 product CTA (full ask)

**5. No false urgency.** "Only 3 spots left" is a lie and readers know it. Don't use it unless it's true.

**6. Soft-sell language that works:**
- "Link in bio"
- "DM me [keyword]"
- "If this sounds like your week..."
- "I built this because I needed it"
- "30 min setup. One-time. Then it runs itself."

## The DM Strategy

DMs are where actual purchases happen on Threads.

**How to trigger DMs:**
- End a post with: "DM me 'AGENT' and I'll send you the details."
- Or: "If this is your situation, DM me — happy to show you what it looks like."

**DM funnel:**
1. User DMs keyword
2. Respond: "Tell me — are you actively searching or thinking about it?"
3. Qualify: how many hours/week, what role, what's the main blocker
4. Send the relevant product link
5. If hesitant: send a specific scenario that matches their situation

## Pinned Comment Strategy

On 30% of your posts: add a pinned promotional comment.
Not in the post body — in the first comment.

This way the post stays value-focused, but anyone who engages deeply sees the CTA.

Example:
Post about job search taking 10 hours/week.
First comment: "If you're tired of searching manually — the Job Search Agent does it for you. $9, runs every morning. Link in profile."

## The Lead Magnet → Product Path

**Free Test as lead magnet:**
- User takes the free calculator
- Sees their number (e.g., "You're spending 11 hours/week on job search")
- Gets redirected to the $9 product page

This is a hard warm-up. Anyone who took the free test is a warm lead.

**On Threads:**
- Post about the pain → CTA to free test
- Free test → email capture (optional) + $9 product offer
- $9 product → $47 upsell

## What NOT to Do (Kills Sales on Threads)

**Over-promoting:**
Posting CTAs on more than 30–40% of your content makes the account feel like an ad feed. People unfollow.

**Generic urgency:**
"Don't miss out!" / "Limited time!" — readers know it's not true. Destroys trust.

**Explaining too much in the CTA:**
The CTA is one line. The product page does the explaining. Keep it short.

**Making the first ask too big:**
Asking a cold follower to buy $47 is too many steps. Cold → free test → $9 → $47.

**Selling without earning it:**
Every CTA needs to follow a post that delivered real value. If the post was low-value, the CTA gets ignored.
