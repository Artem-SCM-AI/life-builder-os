## Core Voice Characteristics

**Direct.** Say the thing. No warm-up sentences. No "have you ever wondered."
"You spend 10 hours a week searching for jobs manually. Here's why that's fixable."

**Personal.** Use "I" and "you." Threads is not a press release.
"I've been where you are. Here's what changed for me."

**Concrete.** Real numbers. Real scenarios. Real friction.
Not: "automation can save you a lot of time"
But: "You check LinkedIn three times a day. The job you want goes to someone who applied in the first 2 hours."

**Spare.** Short sentences. One idea per paragraph. White space is content.
Long paragraphs = scroll past.

**Human.** Vulnerable where it's true. Not performed vulnerability — actual stakes.
"I searched for a job manually. I lost track. I missed follow-ups. I know exactly what that costs you."

## Language Rules

**USE:**
- Short, punchy sentences
- Numbers and specifics (10 hours/week, $9, 30 minutes, 8 interviews)
- Arrow lists for outcomes/results: →
- Real platform names (LinkedIn, Indeed, Notion, Claude Code)
- "I built this because I needed it"
- Second person: "you," "your job search," "your pipeline"

**AVOID:**
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Are you ready to transform your career?"
- "Game-changer," "revolutionary," "next level"
- Passive voice: "it can be automated" → "you can automate it in 30 minutes"
- Excessive hashtags (2–3 max, relevant)
- Emojis as decoration (only if they add clarity, not noise)
- Long intros before the point
- Trailing summaries that repeat what was just said

## Post Structure Principles

**Opening line = the hook.** Must earn the scroll-stop.
Good hook types:
- Number: "You spend 10 hours/week on this."
- Contradiction: "You're applying to the right jobs. You're just applying too late."
- Statement of truth: "Most job searches fail before the interview. Here's why."
- Question (sparingly): "When's the last time a job found you?"

**Body = the proof or the path.**
Deliver on the hook. Give the reader something — a number, a scenario, a step.
Don't save the value for the CTA.

**Ending = direction, not summary.**
Either: a soft CTA, a question that invites reply, or a statement that lands.
Never: a summary of what you just said.

## CTA Rules

**Cold audience** (hasn't engaged yet):
"Find out how many hours your job search is costing you. Free calculator — link in bio."
Zero sales pressure. Pure value/information.

**Warm audience** (engaged, aware of the problem):
"If this sounds like your week — the $9 agent runs this for you every morning."
One step, clear offer, no hype.

**Hot audience** (ready to act):
"30 minutes of setup. Then it runs itself. $9, one-time. Link in bio."
Direct. No fluff. No false urgency.

## Funnel Sequence

Week 1–2: Free Test only. Build awareness of the time problem.
Week 3–4: $9 Agent. "You know the problem. Here's the fix."
Week 5+: $47 Orchestrator. "You want the full system."

Within each week: mix pain posts (60%), solution posts (30%), CTA posts (10%).

## What Artem Is Not Saying

- Not: "You're bad at job searching."
- Not: "If you had discipline you wouldn't need this."
- Not: "AI will replace human judgment."
- Not: "This is easy, why haven't you done it already?"

"The system is broken. Manual job searching is a 1990s process for a 2024 market. You can fix your side of it in 30 minutes."
