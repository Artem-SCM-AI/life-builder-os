# Knowledge Base 1 — Threads Algorithm & Platform Mechanics
*Distilled from 40 research sources. For Claude Projects — Threads Content Machine.*

---

## How the Algorithm Works

Threads has two feeds:
- **For You** — AI-ranked. Your main growth surface.
- **Following** — Reverse chronological. Existing followers only.

The algorithm runs a 3-step loop:
1. **Gather inventory** — Your post competes for eligibility, not automatic distribution.
2. **Read signals** — Likes, replies, reposts, profile taps, follows, time spent.
3. **Make predictions** — What will this user do next? Prioritizes content it predicts will keep them engaged.

**Critical difference from other platforms:** Replies > Likes. The algorithm treats replies as stronger evidence of value than passive likes. A post with 10 replies outranks a post with 50 likes.

---

## The 30-Minute Rule (Most Important)

**The first 30 minutes determine whether a post lives or dies.**

If a post generates replies, reposts, and likes quickly after publishing — the algorithm amplifies it to a broader audience.

If it sits without engagement in the first 30 minutes — it gets deprioritized and may never reach beyond your existing followers, regardless of quality.

**Practical implications:**
- Post when your core audience is active (for US professionals: 7–9am and 12–2pm Eastern)
- Reply to your own posts immediately to start the thread chain
- Ask an easy-to-answer question in the first comment to seed replies
- Having 5–10 people who reliably engage early makes a measurable difference

## How Threads Search Works

Unlike Instagram (hashtag-driven), **Threads scans full post text** — especially the first 1–2 lines.

This makes Threads closer to a text search engine. The opening lines are the primary indexing signal.

**Practical rule:** Put the topic keyword naturally in the first sentence.
- Bad: "There's something I've been thinking about lately..."
- Good: "Job searching takes 10 hours a week. Here's why that's fixable."

Hashtags: use 2–3 relevant ones, but they are NOT the discovery mechanism. Keywords in body text matter more.

---

## Key Ranking Signals (in order of importance)

1. **Replies** — strongest signal
2. **Reposts** — second strongest
3. **Profile taps after seeing post** — high-intent signal
4. **Follows after seeing post** — strong signal
5. **Time spent on post** — matters (longer content that holds attention is rewarded)
6. **Likes** — positive but weakest of the above
7. **Scrolling past** — negative signal (hurts distribution)

**Insight:** Design posts to generate replies, not just likes. End with a question, a "which one are you?" framing, or an invitation to share their experience.

## Content Discovery: Interest Graph, Not Follow Graph

Threads increasingly surfaces content based on **what topics a user engages with**, not just who they follow.

If someone regularly engages with job search or career content — they'll see Artem's posts even without following him.

**This means:** Consistent, topically-focused content builds a topic authority signal. The algorithm starts associating the account with the niche and pushes it to relevant audiences.

## "Dear Algo" Feature (2026)

Users can now tell Threads what they want to see more of in their For You feed.

**Practical implication:** Content that sparks users to say "more like this" gets directional amplification. Posts that start conversations tend to trigger this behavior.

## Posting Cadence

**Minimum for growth:** 2–3 posts/day
**Target (Artem's plan):** 8–10 posts/day (with scheduling tool)

**Best times for US audience (Eastern Time):**
- Morning: 7–9am (commute, coffee, start of day)
- Lunch: 12–2pm
- Evening: 7–9pm

Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday perform best for professional content.
Weekends: lower engagement, but posting keeps algorithm signal alive.

**Key rule:** Consistency > perfection. A modest daily rhythm beats sporadic bursts.

## Profile Optimization

Your bio is the first thing the algorithm uses to categorize you. It must:
- State exactly who you help and what problem you solve in the first line
- Use words your audience actually searches
- Include one clear next step (link in bio to Free Test)

Bad bio: "Helping you work smarter."
Good bio: "I automate job search for busy pros. Free test → [link]"

---

## What Threads Rewards vs. Penalizes

**Rewards:**
- Conversational, human tone
- Posts that invite replies (questions, hot takes, "which one are you?")
- Specific, useful information (not vague)
- Consistency over time
- Engaging ON other relevant posts (comment early, add value)

**Penalizes:**
- Direct links in posts (reduces organic reach — put links in bio or first comment)
- Pure promotional content without value
- Generic corporate language
- Posting then disappearing (no replies to comments)
- Inconsistency (long gaps between posts)

---

## Links Strategy

**Links in posts reduce organic reach.** Meta wants to keep users on platform.

Best practice:
- Put link in bio (Linktree or direct product page)
- In CTA: "link in bio" or "DM me [keyword]"
- If you must link in post — put it in the first reply/comment, not in the post body

---

## The Engagement Loop

Post → Reply to every comment within first hour → Reply chain keeps post alive → Algorithm sees ongoing conversation → Distributes to more users

**One tactic that works:** Add a strong "first comment" to your own post immediately after publishing. This seeds the conversation and gives the algorithm an early signal.
