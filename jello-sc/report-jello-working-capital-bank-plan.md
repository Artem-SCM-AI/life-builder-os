# Working Capital Requirements — Jello Supply Chain
**Accommerce GmbH | Prepared for Bank Submission**

## Overview

This document outlines the key operational metrics that drive the working capital requirements for the Jello product line. All figures are based on current supplier agreements, shipping routes, and sales planning for the DACH market via Shopify.

---

| Metric | Value | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Inventory Days | 30 days | Target safety stock at DACH fulfillment center |
| Production Lead Time | 25 days | Factory-to-ship at Lvmengkang, China |

## Metric Explanations

**Inventory Days — 30 days**
The company maintains a 30-day stock buffer at the German fulfillment center. This provides adequate coverage during transit windows and demand fluctuations while avoiding excess capital tied up in warehousing.

**Production Lead Time — 25 days**
Standard manufacturing lead time at the primary China-based supplier. This covers raw material preparation, production, and quality inspection prior to shipment.

**Shipping Lead Time — 35 days**
Primary shipping route is sea freight from China to Germany (approx. 5 weeks). This is the most cost-efficient mode for bulk orders and is reflected in landed cost calculations.

**Supplier Payment Terms — 0 days**
The current supplier agreement requires 30% deposit upon order placement and 40% prior to loading. The remaining 30% is due 40 days after shipment — which coincides with goods arrival in Germany. As a result, the supplier provides no effective post-receipt credit. The company self-finances 100% of inventory costs during the production and transit cycle.

**Shopify Payout Delay — 3 days**
Revenue collected via the Shopify platform is disbursed to the company within 3 business days under the standard EU merchant payout schedule.

**Average Inventory Purchase Size — €123,000**
Each purchase order covers 21 days of planned sales (3,000 units/day × 21 days = 63,000 units) at a landed cost of approximately €1.95/unit (factory price + sea freight). Orders are placed on a 14-day cycle to maintain continuous stock availability.

**Average Reorder Cycle — 14 days**
Purchase orders are placed every 14 days to align with the sales rhythm and ensure uninterrupted stock coverage given the 60-day total replenishment lead time (production + transit).

## Cash Conversion Cycle

The total cash conversion cycle — from initial supplier payment to revenue receipt — spans approximately **93 days**:

| Stage | Days |
|---|---|
| Production Lead Time | 25 |
| Shipping Lead Time | 35 |
| Inventory Days at Warehouse | 30 |
| Shopify Payout Delay | +3 |
| Supplier Credit | −0 |
| **Total Cash Cycle** | **93 days** |

This 93-day cycle, combined with zero supplier credit, creates a structural working capital gap that requires external financing to maintain uninterrupted operations at planned sales volumes.
