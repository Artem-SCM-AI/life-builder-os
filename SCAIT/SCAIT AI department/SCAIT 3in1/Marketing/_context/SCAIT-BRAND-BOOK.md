## Brand Foundation

### Name & Full Form

**SCAIT** — Supply Chain AI Technology

Pronunciation: /skeɪt/ (one syllable, like "skate")

Always written: **SCAIT** (all caps)  
Never: Scait, scait, S.C.A.I.T.

Product names use full descriptive titles. Three products use branded single-word names (always all-caps):  
**VERIX**, **SPECTRA**, **INSPEX**

All other products use the SC prefix format:  
SC Data Map, SC Stock Radar, SC Delta, SC Supplier Guard, SC 3PL Guard, SC Supplier Desk, SC Forwarder Desk

---

### Positioning Statement

**For e-commerce brand owners managing physical supply chains who are losing money they don't know about — SCAIT is an AI-powered service that catches what manual review misses: hidden supplier overcharges, 3PL billing errors, logistics delays, and quality exceptions — delivered in hours, not weeks.**

Key differentiators:
- Real ROI with verified numbers, not promises
- Manual delivery with AI precision — no integration required
- Built by a supply chain operator, for supply chain operators
- Live in 2 weeks, not 6 months

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

SCAIT visual identity is inspired by Amazon's design language — because the ICP lives in Amazon Seller Central.

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Primary Orange | Amazon Orange | `#FF9900` | CTAs, highlights, accents, alerts |
| Primary Dark | Deep Navy | `#131921` | Backgrounds, headers, slide decks |
| Secondary | White | `#FFFFFF` | Body backgrounds, card backgrounds |
| Text Primary | Near-Black | `#0F1111` | Body text, titles |
| Text Secondary | Gray | `#565959` | Subtext, captions, labels |
| Alert / Critical | Red | `#CC0C39` | ALERT flags, critical exceptions |
| Safe / Positive | Green | `#007600` | Resolved status, positive ROI |

**Rule:** Never use default Tailwind blues, purples, or indigos. The palette is orange + dark navy + white.

## Brand Personality

### The 5 Traits

1. **Precise** — always specific, never vague; numbers over adjectives
2. **Grounded** — operator background, not startup hype; "I built this for myself"
3. **Calm** — confident without noise; the ROI speaks, not the marketing
4. **Useful** — every communication delivers something actionable
5. **Honest** — manual delivery in Phase 1; doesn't pretend to be enterprise software

### The 3 Anti-Traits (what SCAIT is NOT)

1. **Not hype-driven** — no "10x your operations", no rocket emojis, no FOMO language
2. **Not tech-first** — the AI is the method, not the brand; the outcome is the brand
3. **Not generic** — no stock photos of dashboards, no "enterprise software" stock imagery

## Content Formats

### LinkedIn Post Format

```
[Specific incident or number — no intro sentence]

[What happened / what caused it]

[Systemic pattern — why this isn't unique]

[What SCAIT does about it — one clear sentence]

[Optional: "DM me if you want to see what this looks like on your data."]
```

### Sales Deck Slide Structure

1. Title: The number (e.g. "$91,800")
2. What happened (1 sentence)
3. Why it happens (root cause)
4. How SCAIT catches it
5. ROI math (annual service cost vs. first recovery)

### Case Study Format

1. **Company type** (anonymized): e.g., "US Amazon brand, $5M revenue, 3 3PL partners"
2. **The problem**: what was being missed and for how long
3. **The catch**: what SCAIT found and the exact dollar amount
4. **The math**: annual service cost vs. recovered value
