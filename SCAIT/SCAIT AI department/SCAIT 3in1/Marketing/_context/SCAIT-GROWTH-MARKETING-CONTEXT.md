## Go-to-Market Philosophy

**Content is proof, not promotion.**

SCAIT doesn't run ads. It doesn't blast cold emails at scale. It shows real supply chain problems being solved — and attracts buyers who recognize their own situation in the story.

The acquisition loop:
```
Artem posts real SC insight (LinkedIn / YouTube)
    → Prospect recognizes the problem in their own business
    → Prospect reaches out or connects
    → Artem opens with a lead magnet (real analysis of their data)
    → Demo call → Sale
```

Artem is not positioning as a software vendor. He is positioning as **the SC practitioner who built tools other SC practitioners need.**

## Target ICP (for acquisition purposes)

### Primary Persona: "The Overwhelmed Head of SC"

- 5–10 years experience, managing 1–3 person team
- Email = primary tool for supplier and forwarder communication
- Knows they're overpaying somewhere; no time to find it
- **Acquisition channel:** LinkedIn organic, direct DM
- **Lead magnet:** "Send me one supplier email — I'll show you what SCAIT would catch"

### Secondary Persona: "The Founder Who Runs SC Himself"

- $2M–$8M brand, no Head of SC hired yet
- Reads supplier emails personally; invoice review is ad hoc
- Time-constrained, scaling fast, Excel is breaking
- **Acquisition channel:** LinkedIn, Amazon communities (Reddit, Facebook groups)
- **Lead magnet:** "Forward me your last 3PL invoice. I'll find the errors."

### Tertiary Persona: "The COO / Ops Director"

- Buys on ROI, not features
- Doesn't want to understand the tool — wants to see the number
- **Acquisition channel:** LinkedIn (targeted outreach to Ops-title leads)
- **Lead magnet:** ROI calculator with real numbers

## Channel Strategy

### 1. LinkedIn — Primary Channel

**Role:** Brand authority + direct pipeline generation  
**Cadence:** 2 posts/week minimum  
**Format:** Story posts (150–250 words), case study breakdowns, "what I found" hooks

**Content Themes (rotating):**
- Real SC failures and root causes
- Before/after automation with specific numbers
- Supplier invoice case studies — SC Supplier Guard (price change catches, $2,914 overcharge)
- 3PL billing case studies — SC 3PL Guard ($91,800 story, zone error mechanics)
- Inventory monitoring — SC Stock Radar (stockout 30 days earlier, ETD reliability)
- Demurrage case studies — SC Forwarder Desk ($1,400 from 8-day delay)
- Inspection case studies — VERIX (45 min → 10 sec, automated fail alerts)
- "What I found in this invoice / report" thread format
- Counter-intuitive SC insights (e.g., "manual review catches ~50% of 3PL errors")

**Artem's positioning on LinkedIn:**
- SC practitioner with a proven track record → not a startup founder promoting software
- Posts speak to SC professionals as peers
- Personal brand doubles as SCAIT acquisition and job search brand (compatible positioning)

**Outreach DM System (Dream 100 method):**
- 10 personalized connection requests/week
- Follow-up message 2–3 days after accept
- Lead magnet in every first pitch (never pitch a call as first ask)
- Weekly targets:
  - Week 1–2: 20 connection requests sent
  - Week 3: First demo calls booked
  - Week 4: 3+ calls/week
  - Week 5: First close

**Prospect Scoring (use before sending DM):**

| Signal | Points |
|--------|--------|
| Revenue $5M–$20M (Storeleads, LinkedIn) | 2 |
| Active Amazon FBA importer | 2 |
| SC team < 10 people | 2 |
| Email-based SC ops (no ERP) | 1 |
| Posts about SC problems (last 30 days) | 1 |
| Recent stockout / audit / SC hire | 1 |
| Uses 1+ 3PL partners | 1 |

**8–10 = Dream 100 priority.** Send immediately.  
**5–7 = Nurture.** Connect, add to sequence.  
**< 5 = Skip.**

## Lead Magnet System

The only goal of the first outreach message: get the prospect to raise their hand.

Never pitch a call first. Always lead with a free deliverable.

### Lead Magnets by Product

| Product | Lead Magnet |
|---------|------------|
| SC Supplier Desk | "Forward me one supplier email — I'll show you what SCAIT would have caught" |
| SC 3PL Guard | "Send me your last 3PL invoice — I'll run a quick line-by-line check" |
| SC Supplier Guard | "Send me a recent supplier invoice and PO — I'll show you the 3-way match" |
| SC Forwarder Desk | "Share a recent forwarder thread — I'll show you what ETA shifts you missed" |
| SC Stock Radar | "Give me 2 weeks of inventory + velocity data — I'll find your stockout risk signals" |
| VERIX | "Forward me your last inspection report — I'll show you what VERIX would have done with it in 60 seconds" |

**Rule:** Don't build the deliverable until they reply. Saves hours of wasted effort.

## Follow-Up Sequence (3-Step)

**Day 1:** Original outreach (personalized hook + lead magnet)

**Day 3:**
> still have [lead magnet] pulled up for you. mind if i send it over?

**Day 7:**
> last follow up — [lead magnet] is ready whenever you are. worth a look?

Same message, three times. Don't add information. Don't apologize for following up.

## Content Production System

### LinkedIn Post Framework (weekly rhythm)

**Monday:** Story post — real SC failure/incident with a number  
**Thursday:** Insight post — systemic problem or counter-intuitive finding

**Story post structure:**
1. Specific incident or number (first line — no preamble)
2. What happened
3. Why it happens at most SC teams
4. What SCAIT does about it
5. Optional: soft CTA ("DM me if you want to run this on your data")

**Do not:**
- Start with a question
- Use generic hooks ("Most brand owners don't know this…")
- End with a question to drive engagement (it reads desperate)
- Use emojis as decoration

## Competitive Positioning

SCAIT does not name competitors in marketing. The positioning is built entirely on the cost of inaction:

- Manual review misses ~50% of 3PL invoice errors
- Supplier price changes are buried in email threads
- Demurrage accumulates in detection gaps

**The competition is not other software — it is spreadsheets, email, and human attention.**

The buyer choice is not SCAIT vs. Tool X. It is SCAIT vs. continuing to lose $2,914 on a single order.
