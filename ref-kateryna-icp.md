# ICP — Kateryna's Ideal Client Profile
## Source: Interview #1, 08.06.2026

---

## Primary Profile — Woman in Crisis (30–45)

**Who she is:**
- Age 30–45
- Has a family: husband, children (often teenagers or grown)
- Has work or career of some kind — but it doesn't feel like enough
- Outwardly "has everything": apartment, husband, children, income

**What's happening in her life:**
- Already hit a crisis — not approaching it, already in it
- Wants to change something but doesn't know how or where to start
- Doesn't know how to be with her husband (distant, conflict, no connection)
- Doesn't know how to be with her children (they "climb on her head" or she can't reach them)
- Doesn't know who she is outside of her roles (wife, mother, employee)
- Lost a sense of what she wants for herself

**What she says (her words):**
- "I don't know where to start"
- "I just don't care [about myself]"
- "I undervalue myself. I don't hear myself." — (arrives at this as a conclusion)
- Demands too much from herself even when she's actually doing a lot

**What she feels:**
- Confusion
- Uncertainty
- Dissatisfaction with herself and her life
- Dependency on a partner's or family's opinion
- Exhausted from serving everyone else

**Financial profile (for 10,000–30,000 packages):**
- Either has her own good income/business
- Or has a partner who earns well and is ready to invest in herself
- Thinks in larger frames — her ceiling isn't 20–30k/month
- Doesn't have internal block around "spending money on herself"
- Has already spent money on something similar before (courses, coaching) or this is her first serious investment in herself

**Trigger for reaching out:**
- Breaking point — something collapsed (relationship, health, self-respect)
- Usually comes by recommendation (trust transfer)
- Comes in the moment when she WANTS to resolve the issue — not drifting, actively seeking

---

## Secondary Profile — Young Woman (20–25)

**Who she is:**
- Age 20–25
- No serious relationship yet, or first relationships
- Educated or finishing education
- Lives independently or semi-independently

**What she comes with:**
- Low internal self-worth — the core issue
- No self-sufficiency (often depends emotionally on others' approval)
- Questions about identity: who am I, what do I want
- Often doesn't know how to say no

**What's different from the 30–45 profile:**
- Hasn't accumulated the "wreckage" yet — comes earlier
- More open, faster pace of work is possible
- Questions are less tangled but just as real

---

## Developing Profile — Man in Internal Crisis (35+)

**Who he is:**
- Age 35+
- Outwardly realized: income, career, family or partner
- Looks "fine" to the outside world

**What's happening inside:**
- Chronically tired — but can't name from what
- Internal crisis he doesn't have words for
- Feeling that something is off — not a specific problem, just "something isn't right"
- "Everything is good but something feels past"
- Not in acute pain — in a slow dissatisfaction

**Why he comes:**
- Not in a breakdown moment — more in a "I want to understand myself" moment
- Rarely comes on his own; more likely comes by someone's strong recommendation

**Note:** This profile is currently underrepresented in Kateryna's practice. The Threads account can become the bridge.

---

## Who Is NOT the Client

- Minors (under 18)
- Military with active PTSD
- People with severe clinical disorders (autism, ADHD)
- People looking for a one-time "fix" with no intention to do consistent work
- People who come to be told what to do (looking for a director, not a therapist)
- Young guys without life experience ("not interesting to work with")

---

## What the Ideal Client Is Ready For

- Consistent work — not a one-time consultation
- Slow process — understands results take months, not weeks
- Doing work between sessions (homework, reflection)
- Being honest with themselves
- Investing money as a signal of commitment to themselves
- Hearing direct feedback — including uncomfortable truths

---

## Key Insight for Content

The ICP doesn't come looking for "therapy."  
She comes looking for **permission to change** and **proof that it's possible.**  
The content must give her that 1% of hope — "maybe I can too."
