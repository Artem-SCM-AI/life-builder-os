# What Can I Automate in My Work?
### 25-Question Personal Automation Audit

*Works for any profession — business owner, freelancer, manager, or specialist*

---

## How This Works

Think about your typical workweek — all the tasks you do regularly.
For each question, rate how often it applies to you:

**Frequency scale:**
- **0** — Never / doesn't apply to me
- **1** — A few times a month
- **2** — Every week
- **3** — This is part of my daily routine

Score each category separately at the end.
The highest-scoring categories = your biggest automation opportunities.

## What Your Highest Category Means

**A — Repetitive Tasks**
You're doing the same work on repeat. The steps don't change — only the details do. This is the most straightforward automation win: you describe the pattern once, the machine does it forever.

*Common in: any admin role, solo business owners, operations managers, service businesses.*

---

**B — Reminders & Follow-Ups**
You're spending real mental energy keeping track of what needs to be sent, confirmed, or chased. Automated sequences handle this without you having to remember or initiate anything.

*Common in: service providers, freelancers, sales roles, client-facing businesses of any kind.*

---

**C — Numbers & Money**
You're doing accounting by hand — manually moving numbers between systems, counting cash, reconciling differences. Every minute spent here is a minute not spent on actual business decisions.

*Common in: small business owners, bar and restaurant owners, self-employed professionals, logistics operators.*

---

**D — Paperwork & Documents**
You're filling in structured information manually when you already have that information somewhere. Document automation and AI extraction can handle most of this with higher accuracy and zero effort.

*Common in: anyone who deals with invoices, contracts, forms, trip logs, client files, or compliance documents.*

---

**E — Coordination & Scheduling**
You are the connection between people and systems that don't talk to each other. This is exhausting because it requires constant attention with no real creative value. Automated workflows and scheduling tools eliminate the back-and-forth entirely.

*Common in: managers, coordinators, salon owners, dispatch operations, agencies, anyone with a team.*

---

**F — Monitoring & Alerts**
You discover problems by looking — which means you discover them late. Automated monitoring watches your key metrics continuously and alerts you the moment something goes wrong, before the damage compounds.

*Common in: business owners, operations roles, anyone responsible for stock, cash, capacity, or delivery performance.*

## The Three Questions That Matter Most

After you see your highest-scoring categories, ask yourself:

**1. What would I stop doing if this were automated?**
List the exact tasks. Make them concrete.

**2. What does one hour of this manual work actually cost you?**
Your hourly rate × hours per week × 52. Most people are shocked.

**3. What is the first thing I'd automate if someone set it up for me?**
That answer is your starting point.

---

## One Rule to Remember

> If you can finish this sentence — *"Every time X happens, I do Y"* —
> that task can be automated.

The goal isn't to replace yourself.
It's to stop doing work that a machine can do better, faster, and without forgetting.
