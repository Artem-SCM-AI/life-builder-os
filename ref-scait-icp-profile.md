# SCAIT — Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

> Last updated: April 2026

---

## Primary ICP: "Frozen in Spreadsheets"

E-commerce brand doing $2M–$30M/year. Manages suppliers and 3PLs via email and Excel. Financial leakage exists — nobody knows how much.

---

## Firmographics

| Parameter | Range |
|---|---|
| Business model | Amazon FBA / FBM, private label, DTC e-commerce |
| Revenue | $2M–$30M/year (sweet spot: $5M–$15M) |
| SKUs | 20–200 active SKUs |
| Suppliers | Any count — 1 supplier with high order volume qualifies |
| Sourcing | China, Asia, US, EU — location is not a filter |
| 3PL / warehouse | 1–3 partners |
| SC team | 1–5 people (often Ops Manager + coordinator) |
| Geography | US, Canada, UK, EU |
| Company age | 3–10 years |

---

## Buyer Personas

### Persona A — "The Overwhelmed Head of SC"
- 5–10 years of experience, managing a team of 1–3
- Responsible for everything: suppliers, 3PL, quality, inventory
- Email is the primary tool for supplier and forwarder communication
- Says: *"I know we're overpaying somewhere, but there's no time to dig"*
- Pain: manual reporting, missed follow-ups, surprise charges from 3PL

### Persona B — "The Founder Who Runs SC Himself"
- $2M–$8M brand, hasn't hired a Head of SC yet
- Handles supplier emails and invoice checks personally
- Time is the scarcest resource
- Says: *"I need to automate SC but don't know where to start"*
- Pain: email chaos, no system, growing too fast for spreadsheets

### Persona C — "The COO / Ops Director"
- Views SC as a cost center, wants KPIs and reports
- Not deep in day-to-day details, but owns margin outcomes
- Buys on ROI, not features
- Says: *"Show me the payback"*
- Pain: invisible costs, no audit trail, manual errors slipping through

## Psychographics

**They believe:**
- Their suppliers and 3PLs are mostly honest — "we'd know if something was off"
- Excel can scale a little longer
- Automation is complex, expensive, and takes months

**The reality:**
- Suppliers regularly test pricing boundaries — price bumps appear in email threads, not spreadsheets
- 3PL invoices contain errors that pass through undetected at manual review rates
- SCAIT automation runs in 2 weeks, not 6 months

---

## Trigger Events — When They Are Ready to Buy

- Just survived a stockout or major supply chain disruption
- Received a 3PL invoice that "doesn't add up"
- Supplier raised a price at the last moment
- Hired a new Head of SC or Ops Manager (looking for quick wins)
- Company is scaling and Excel is breaking down
- Preparing for Q4 / peak season
- Ran an internal audit and found gaps
- Had a quality dispute they couldn't document properly

---

## Disqualifiers — NOT Our Client

| Signal | Why Not |
|---|---|
| Revenue < $1M | Insufficient transaction volume and budget |
| Dropshipper with no own inventory | No real supply chain to manage |
| Large enterprise with SAP / Oracle / WMS | Already automated at this layer |
| Pure digital products | No physical supply chain |
