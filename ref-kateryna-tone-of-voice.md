# Kateryna — Tone of Voice
## Source: Interview #1, 08.06.2026

---

## The Voice in One Sentence

Warm, direct, and a little funny — like a friend who happens to know exactly what's going on with you and isn't afraid to say it.

---

## What the Voice IS

**Warm and close**
Closeness is non-negotiable. "You have to get used to closeness — without it there's no work and no result."
Not clinical. Not detached. Not "professionally distant."

**Direct — calls things by their names**
"I'm truly straightforward. I call things by their names."
- If the behavior is unacceptable — says so
- If a child has climbed on your head — says so
- If you've exhausted yourself — says so
- Does NOT tell people what to do — but names what IS

**Humorous — genuinely, not performatively**
Son says she runs a stand-up every session.
Humor comes from what she's actually saying, not from trying to be funny.
"On the edge" — not sharp, not cutting, but not safe and bland either.

**Non-judgmental about people, honest about situations**
Doesn't evaluate people as good or bad.
Does evaluate situations, behaviors, patterns clearly.

**Pragmatic**
About aggressive comments: "Unpleasant. But so what? They're boosting activity."
About results: "I give what I can give. The person takes what they can take."
No victim mentality. No over-dramatization.

**Grounded in specificity**
Doesn't deal in abstract concepts. Translates everything into real life.
Uses concrete images (hair growth, gym, trainer).

---

## What the Voice is NOT

**Not a motivational coach**
No "you're incredible," no "believe in yourself," no "you can do it."
No toxic positivity. No empty encouragement.

**Not a classic therapist**
Doesn't speak in clinical language.
Doesn't create emotional distance.
Doesn't hide behind "professional objectivity."

**Not a victim**
Doesn't position clients as victims of their circumstances.
Doesn't feed self-pity.
"Suffering is not mandatory. Being a victim your whole life is not your destiny."

**Not a lecturer**
Doesn't explain what clients should feel or think.
Doesn't moralize.
"I'm not a teacher. I don't grade here."

**Not overly spiritual/esoteric**
Despite training in metaphorical cards and numerology — the public voice is grounded and practical.

---

## Forbidden Words and Phrases

| Forbidden | Why |
|---|---|
| "Неймовірна / неймовірний" | Explicitly banned. "Everything with неймовірний is forbidden for us." |
| Generic coaching phrases ("believe in yourself", "you're worthy") | Sounds inauthentic, not her voice |
| Clinical jargon (unless explained simply) | Creates distance |
| "You should..." / "You must..." | She doesn't prescribe — she reflects |
| Excessive exclamation marks / hype | Not her energy |

## Humor Style

- Self-aware humor about her own directness
- Observational — about patterns she sees in people
- Never at the client's expense
- Never dark or cutting
- The kind of humor that makes you laugh AND think
- Works well in Threads format — punchy, unexpected turn

## Emotional Register per Content Type

| Content type | Register |
|---|---|
| Transformation stories | Warm, grounded, slightly poetic at the end |
| Hard truths about patterns | Direct, clear, no softening — but not cold |
| "You're not alone" posts | Close, personal, almost whispered |
| Myth-busting about therapy | Pragmatic, a little ironic |
| Call to action | Light, no pressure, one sentence max |
| Humor posts | Punchy, unexpected, warm aftertaste |

## The Publishing Test

Before posting, ask:
1. Does it sound like something she'd actually say to a client?
2. Is there at least one concrete, specific thing — not just an abstract idea?
3. Does it avoid the forbidden word list?
4. Does it leave the reader with something — an emotion, a thought, a small shift?
5. Is the CTA (if any) one sentence, no pressure?

If yes to all five — publish.
