# Can YOU Launch This Idea?
### 25-Question Founder-Idea Fit Assessment

*The idea might be great. The question is whether YOU are the right person to build it.*

---

## How This Works

This test is not about your idea.
It's about you — your experience, resources, commitment, and track record.

Answer each question honestly about your current situation.

**Scoring scale:**
- **0** — Not true at all / I have none of this
- **1** — Slightly true / minimal
- **2** — Somewhat true / partial
- **3** — Mostly true / strong
- **4** — Completely true / fully in place

## Your Result

| Score | Verdict | What it means |
|-------|---------|---------------|
| **0–35** | Wrong fit | This is not your problem, your market, or your time. A great idea with the wrong founder fails. Find a problem where you have an unfair advantage. |
| **36–55** | Developing fit | Promising signals but real gaps. Identify your two weakest blocks and fill them before you build. Gaps in blocks 1–2 are harder to fix than gaps in blocks 3–4. |
| **56–75** | Good fit | You have the right ingredients. The idea needs aggressive validation now — not more planning. Talk to 10 customers this week. |
| **76–100** | Strong fit | You have founder-idea fit. Your experience, network, and drive match the problem. The risk is over-planning instead of launching. |

## How to Read Your Block Scores

**Block 1 (Domain knowledge) is the hardest to fake.**
You can learn to sell. You can hire technical skills. But domain credibility — knowing the world your customer lives in — takes years and cannot be shortcut. If Block 1 is low, consider pivoting to a problem you know deeply.

**Block 2 (Felt the pain) is the best predictor.**
Research from Y Combinator and NFX consistently shows that founders who personally experienced the problem they're solving outperform those who identified it analytically. "I had this problem" beats "I saw a market gap."

**Block 3 (Execution) is what separates ideas from businesses.**
Everyone has ideas. The question is whether you finish things. A low Block 3 score is a red flag — not because you can't learn to execute, but because you'd be learning while burning time and money.

**Block 4 (Resources) is more fixable than it feels.**
Low runway can be managed with part-time progression. Missing technical skills can be partnered or outsourced. The one resource you can't borrow: access to your first customers. If Block 4 Q16 is low, that's the priority.

**Block 5 (Durability) determines whether you quit.**
Most businesses that eventually succeed required 2–4 years of difficult work before the breakthrough. If your motivation depends on early traction, Block 5 weakness is existential.

---

## The One Question That Overrides Everything

> **Why are YOU the right person to solve this — and not someone else?**

If you can't answer that clearly, investors won't fund it, partners won't join it, and customers won't trust it. Your answer to that question is your real pitch.
