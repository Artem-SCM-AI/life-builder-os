## Voice Rules for Threads (Platform-Specific)

**Write like you'd text a smart friend. Not like a press release.**

- Short sentences dominate
- One idea per post
- Personal "I" and direct "you"
- No corporate filler
- Emojis: fine, but sparse. 0–2 per post. Not as decoration, only if they add meaning.
- Hashtags: 2–3 max. Relevant ones. Not for discovery (keywords in text do more).

**The test for any sentence:** "Would I say this to a real person over coffee?"
If no → cut it.

## Engagement Rules

**Reply to every comment within the first hour.** This is not optional. The reply chain keeps the post alive in the algorithm.

**Engaging on OTHER people's posts is growth, not chit-chat.** Reply to larger accounts in your niche. Add something useful: an example, a specific fact, a clarifying question. "Agree" as a comment is useless. A specific insight that adds to the post gets seen by that creator's entire engaged audience.

**Training the algorithm through engagement:** If you consistently comment under posts about job search and career automation, Threads learns your niche and serves your posts to users in that interest graph.

## Content Batching (Practical Execution)

**For 8–10 posts/day:**
Batch-write 50–70 posts per week in one or two sessions.
Schedule via Buffer or similar tool.
Leave time to reply to comments daily (20–30 min/day engagement session).

**Weekly rhythm:**
- Mon–Sat: posts run on schedule
- Daily: 20–30 min to reply to all comments
- Sunday: review what worked, note top-performing hooks and formats, update content plan

**What to track weekly:**
- Which hooks got the most replies
- Which topics generated the most profile taps
- Which posts people screenshotted or reposted
- Which CTAs generated DMs

## What "Consistency" Actually Means

Consistency is not about volume. It's about **predictability + presence**.

The algorithm needs to see activity. Your audience needs to know what to expect from you.

**Minimum viable consistency:** 2 posts/day, every day, for 30+ days.
**Target for Artem's 1-year challenge:** 8–10 posts/day.

The first 30 days: focus on reaching and recognizing patterns.
The second 30 days: double down on what worked.

## Posting Time Guidelines (US Audience)

Best times for US/Canada professional audience:
- **7:00–9:00am ET** — commute/coffee, highest morning attention
- **12:00–2:00pm ET** — lunch break
- **7:00–9:00pm ET** — evening wind-down

Best days: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday.
Worst days: Saturday (low engagement), Sunday mornings.

**Important:** These are starting points. After 30 days of posting, your own analytics will tell you when YOUR audience is active. Trust your data over generic benchmarks.

## Profile: The Non-Negotiable Foundation

Before any content strategy works, the profile must convert.

**Bio formula:**
[Who you help] + [What problem] + [Next step]

> "I automate job search for busy pros. Free test → [link]"

What the bio must NOT be:
- Vague: "Helping you succeed"
- Passive: "Sharing thoughts on automation"
- About you: "Former SC head, now building tools"

The bio is for the algorithm (what niche am I in?) and the visitor (is this for me?). Both need clarity.

## The "Dear Algo" Feature (2026)

Threads users can now explicitly tell the algorithm what they want to see more of.

**Practical implication:** When your post triggers someone to actively want "more like this," the algorithm gives it directional amplification.

**How to earn "more like this" reactions:**
- Posts that are so specific and accurate that readers feel seen
- Posts that name an uncomfortable truth about a situation the reader is in
- Posts that offer a genuinely useful insight they want to return to

## What Kills Momentum

- Posting and disappearing (not replying to comments)
- Promotional posts without value context
- Inconsistent frequency (active for a week, gone for two)
- Writing in long paragraphs (no one reads walls of text on mobile)
- Copying other platforms' content verbatim (Threads has a distinct culture)
- Chasing generic virality instead of niche depth

---

## What Builds Long-Term Authority

- Being the person who consistently explains a specific problem better than anyone else
- Showing up daily with a recognizable voice
- Replying thoughtfully to your own comments
- Engaging genuinely on adjacent creators' content
- Rotating topics but staying on-niche (job search automation for professionals)
- Being honest about what the product can and can't do (trust = sales)
